What this is

One free AI account. Your own materials. Properly formatted documents in minutes.

Claude is a free AI assistant made by Anthropic. Within Claude, you create a Project: a private space where you paste a one-time instruction and upload your teaching materials (e-notes or a topic list). From then on, every document Claude generates for that project follows the exact rules in the instruction: your school's name, the right formatting, the right curriculum content, and the right structure. Every single time, for any teacher who uses it.

Always true
  • Completely free to sign up and use.
  • Works for any subject and any class, you are not limited to one.
  • One-time setup per tool; after that, one sentence generates a full document.
  • Output is always a proper Word (.docx) file, ready to download, print, or submit.
  • Yoruba subject content is generated entirely in pure Yoruba, with full tonal diacritical marks.
  • Claude asks you to confirm missing details (term, subject, class, week) instead of guessing.
Good to know
  • Each request varies its question selection and phrasing, running the same request twice won't give you an identical paper.
  • The Lesson Note Generator can look up your state's Ministry of Education school calendar to work out the real dates for a given week, including mid-term breaks.
  • A single Project can hold materials for as many subjects and classes as you teach, you don't need a new project for every subject.
  • Not happy with a result? Just reply in the same chat, e.g. "Redo Section A" or "Make the theory questions harder."

Before you begin: the Exam & Revision Generator is not yet tailored to English Language or Mathematics (Mathematics especially, because of diagrams). Hold off on those two subjects until a tailored version is released, every other subject is fully supported.

The two tools

Pick the setup that matches what you need this week.

Exam, Marking Guide & Revision File Generator

Type a term, subject and class, and choose whether you want an examination (a 70-mark question paper plus a matching marking guide, produced as two separate Word files) or a revision file (summary notes and answered practice questions in one Word file, for students to self-study with).

  • Section A: 30 objective questions, 30 marks
  • Section B: theory questions, 40 marks
  • Third Term exams automatically blend all three terms in a fixed ratio
Open this tool's full guide →

Weekly Lesson Note Generator

Type a subject, class and week number, and Claude fetches your state's school calendar, works out the real dates, and writes a complete lesson note booklet, objectives, entry behaviour, presentation steps, evaluation and assignment, for every period in that week.

  • 3 periods a week for most subjects, 4 for Mathematics & English Language
  • Works from full e-notes, or from a topic list alone
  • Full pure-Yoruba output for the Yoruba subject

Before you start

What you'll need

Device

A smartphone (Android or iPhone) or a computer/laptop. Nothing else is required.

Connection

An internet connection, just for the few minutes you're generating or setting things up.

Materials

Your e-notes for the subject and class (all three terms), or a scheme of work / topic list if you don't have e-notes.

Setting everything up the first time takes about 15 minutes, and you only do it once per tool. After that, generating a document takes under two minutes.

A note to supervisors and school administrators. This tool does not replace the teacher. It removes the burden of repetitive paperwork so more energy goes into actual teaching, student engagement, and classroom management.

Every document produced is structured, curriculum-compliant, professionally formatted, and consistent across every teacher and subject, meaning easier supervision, easier review, and a higher standard of documentation across the school. Teachers are not stepping away from their professional responsibilities here; they're fulfilling them at a higher, more consistent standard.
