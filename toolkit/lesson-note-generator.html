Weekly Lesson Note Generator

Generates a complete weekly lesson note booklet, objectives, presentation steps, evaluation and assignments for every period, for any subject and class, from your e-notes or from a topic list alone.

40Min per period
3 to 4Periods / week

What it does

For any subject, class and term, a single Project can hold materials for as many subjects and classes as you teach. Claude works out which materials belong to your request, fetches your state's official school calendar to compute the real dates for the requested week (including mid-term breaks), and writes a complete booklet covering every period in that week.

Every period includes: Behavioural Objectives, Entry Behaviour, Instructional Materials, Reference Materials, Introduction, Presentation (minimum 4 steps), Evaluation, Conclusion, and Assignment.

Two supported modes, use whichever you have

Mode A, Enote mode

You already have full e-notes with lesson content, definitions, examples, diagrams and exercises. Claude draws content primarily from your e-note, then the optional textbook, then credible online sources.

Mode B, Topic-list mode

You only have a weekly topic list (subject, class, session, topics, no content). Claude builds the full lesson content from credible online educational sources and standard textbooks.

Mode detection is automatic and applies per Subject + Class. One subject can use Mode A while another uses Mode B, inside the very same Project.

How to ask for it

Always state the Subject and Class, plus the Week number. The Term is optional, Claude works it out by checking which set of materials contains that week number.

  • "Generate Week 3, Physics, SS2"
  • "Generate Week 5, 2nd Term, Chemistry, SS1"
  • "Generate Week 1, Third Term, Basic Science, JSS1"

Before producing the document, Claude confirms the Subject, Class, Term, Week number, computed dates, and any mid-term break or public holiday affecting that week.

Create one Project dedicated to lesson notes, name it something like Lesson Note Generator. One Project can hold materials for as many subjects and classes as you need.

2

Edit and paste the instruction

Open your Project's Project Instructions field. Before pasting the text below, edit the three placeholder lines near the top, then paste the whole thing in and save.

  • {SCHOOL_NAME}, your school's full official name. Appears on every lesson note booklet, and overrides any school name found in an uploaded e-note.
  • {STATE}, the Nigerian state whose Ministry of Education school calendar Claude should fetch for computing weekly dates, e.g. "Oyo State."
  • {LOCATION_CONTEXT}: a short description of your students' everyday environment, so examples and the Entry Behaviour section feel relatable, e.g. "a rural community in Iseyin."

How the dates are computed

  1. Claude identifies the official resumption date for the relevant term (the Monday of Week 1).
  2. Week n Monday = Term Resumption Monday + (n − 1) × 7 days.
  3. Week n Friday = Week n Monday + 4 days.
  4. If a mid-term break falls on the Thursday to Friday of that week, the lesson week runs Monday to Wednesday only, and the break is noted explicitly.
  5. If a mid-term break covers the whole computed week, Claude re-maps to the first teaching week after the break and tells you.
  6. If a public holiday falls within the week, it's noted in the header, and lesson notes are still generated for the remaining teaching days.

Dates are always fetched fresh from your state's Ministry of Education calendar, never hardcoded.
